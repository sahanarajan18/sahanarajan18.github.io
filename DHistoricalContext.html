• Born in French city of La Haye en Touraine, called “Descartes” in his honor today.
  • 11 years: Entered Jesuit college
  • 20 years: Earned law degree to please father, who was a high-court judge
  • Descartes never practiced law.
  • After graduation, he left all study and became military engineer. He believed military life was idle and cruel but did it to be able to travel around the world and to discover “great book of the world”.
  • Sold all his possessions and invested the funds, along with money from patrons and inheritance: This gave him privilege to travel and study most of his life.
  • Specific direction to study came when he dreamt of becoming scientist and philosopher on 3 occasions during his stay at Europe
  • At this point, he decided to follow his desire.
  • He became an author and was most fruitful during his 20-year residence at Dutch Republic: He used to routinely stay in bed writing until noon.
  • He cared for his privacy and shifted homes to protect it. But he became well-known through his works.
  • He was well-known in science and mathematics, in philosophy.
  • He never married. He did father children. He rationalised that he had never taken a vow of chastity.
  • He was invited to become teacher to 22-year old Queen Christina of Sweden: Descartes on a radically changed daily routine where he had to get up at 5am, instruct the demanding queen in philosophy.
  • Died at 53 due to pneumonia: Said that it was due to pressure and inconvenient schedule + maybe he contracted the disease while taking care of a French ambassador with pneumonia.
