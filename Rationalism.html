1. Rationalism
  • 1. Knowledge is acquired through reason, without aid of senses
  • 2. Example: Mathematical knowledge
  • 3. History: Plato is one of the popular rationalists and others who followed him. Mid-17TH century saw rationalism developing with add-on of the idea that our mental concepts are innate/inborn and from these concepts, we can deduce other truths with complete certainty. Scholars of continental European countries of France, Germany and Netherland followed it: Continental Rationalism. Followers include Rene Descartes, Nicholas Malebranche, Baruch Spinoza, Gottfried Willhelm Leibniz.
